What is Online Wellbeing

Online wellbeing is about how digital life affects your mood, confidence, focus, and overall mental and emotional health.

What It Includes

  • Managing mental and emotional health in the digital world
  • Balancing screen time with offline activities

Safer Online Space

  • Staying safe from harmful content and interactions
  • Making digital habits work for you, not against you

Why It Matters

Too much time online or exposure to unhealthy content can affect sleep, concentration, confidence, and mental wellbeing.

Possible Effects

  • Excessive screen time can harm sleep and focus
  • Social media can affect self-esteem and confidence

Wider Impact

  • Online abuse and negativity can impact mental health
  • Unhealthy habits can start to affect daily life offline too

Healthy Online Habits

Small, consistent habits can make online life feel healthier, calmer, and much more manageable.

Daily Habits

  • Set time limits for apps and browsing
  • Take regular breaks from screens
  • Follow positive and supportive accounts

Healthier Choices

  • Mute or unfollow harmful content
  • Prioritise face-to-face interactions when possible

Managing Stress Online

If online spaces are making you feel drained or overwhelmed, it is worth stepping back and putting support in place.

What Can Help

  • Avoid arguments or toxic debates online
  • Use mindfulness or relaxation apps

Support Steps

  • Talk to trusted friends or family if feeling overwhelmed
  • Seek professional help if online activity impacts daily life

Examples of Online Wellbeing Choices

These examples are split clearly so the less healthy habits and better alternatives are easier to compare.

Less Healthy Choices

  • Spending 6+ hours daily on social media
  • Comparing yourself to unrealistic online posts
  • Engaging in online conflicts

Better Choices

  • Limiting notifications to reduce distraction
  • Following wellbeing and mental health pages
