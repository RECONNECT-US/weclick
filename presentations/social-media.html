What is Social Media Safety?

  • Protecting personal information on platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok.
  • Avoiding harmful interactions with strangers.
  • Controlling who can see and comment on your posts.
Social media safety is about using these platforms in a way that protects your privacy, your reputation, and your peace of mind.

Why Social Media Safety Matters

  • Oversharing can lead to identity theft, harassment, or scams.
  • Private information can be used maliciously.
  • Posts can affect future employment or education opportunities.

Common Risks

  • Identity theft
  • Harassment
  • Scams and misuse of personal details

Why Awareness Helps

  • Keeps your information more private
  • Reduces risk from strangers
  • Helps protect your future reputation

How to Stay Safe on Social Media

  • Adjust privacy settings for each account.
  • Only accept friend or follow requests from people you know.
  • Avoid sharing personal information like your phone number or address.
  • Think before posting photos or videos.
  • Report or block inappropriate accounts.
A few stronger privacy habits can make social media feel safer and much easier to manage.

Examples of Social Media Risks

These examples are split clearly so the risky behaviour and the safer alternatives are easy to compare.

Risky Examples

  • Posting your home address publicly
  • Accepting friend requests from strangers
  • Sharing travel plans while away from home

Safer Examples

  • Using strong privacy settings
  • Only posting general updates

Key Takeaways

  • Protect your personal information.
  • Only connect with people you know.
  • Think before posting.
  • Report and block abusive accounts.
What you share online can travel fast, so stronger privacy habits matter more than most people think.
