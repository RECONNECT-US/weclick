What is Misinformation & Fake News

Not all false information is shared for the same reason. Some is spread carelessly, while some is created deliberately to mislead people.

Key Definitions

  • Misinformation: false information spread without intent to harm
  • Disinformation: false information spread deliberately to mislead

Fake News

  • Fabricated stories presented as real news
  • Often shared quickly online without proper checking

Why It Matters

False or misleading information can have real effects on people’s decisions, trust, and safety, especially when it spreads quickly.

Possible Harm

  • Can influence opinions and decisions unfairly
  • May cause panic, confusion, or mistrust

Wider Impact

  • Can damage reputations
  • May spread harmful stereotypes or false beliefs

How to Spot Fake News

Good fact-checking habits can stop you from being misled by dramatic, poorly sourced, or emotionally manipulative content.

Things to Check

  • Check the source — is it reliable?
  • Look for sensational headlines or poor spelling

Safer Habits

  • Verify information with trusted news outlets
  • Be cautious of content shared on social media without evidence

What to Do When You See Fake News

You do not need to argue with everything you see online, but you should avoid helping false information spread any further.

Immediate Actions

  • Do not share it further
  • Report misleading posts on social media

Better Next Steps

  • Share accurate information from trusted sources
  • Talk to others about verifying before sharing
