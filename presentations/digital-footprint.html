What is a Digital Footprint

Your digital footprint is the trail of information you leave behind online. It becomes part of how other people and organisations see you.

What It Includes

  • Social media posts
  • Photos and videos
  • Comments and messages

Why It Matters

  • Browsing history can be part of it too
  • It forms your online identity
  • It shapes your reputation over time

Why It Matters

What is online about you can affect how people judge you, trust you, and decide whether to offer you opportunities.

Possible Risks

  • Employers, schools, and others may check your online presence
  • Harmful content can damage opportunities

Possible Benefits

  • Positive content can strengthen trust
  • Good posts can improve credibility and reputation

How to Manage It

The best way to manage your digital footprint is to be deliberate: pause before posting, review what is public, and keep private details protected.

Before You Post

  • Think before you post or share
  • Keep personal information private
  • Share positive, respectful content

Ongoing Checks

  • Review privacy settings on all accounts
  • Delete old or harmful content

Key Takeaways

Your online reputation is not just about avoiding problems. It is also about building a digital presence that reflects you well.

What to Remember

  • Your digital footprint can feel permanent
  • Employers and organisations may check it

What to Do

  • Be mindful of what you share online
  • Aim to build a positive reputation
