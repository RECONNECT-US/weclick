What are Scams & Phishing

  • Scams are tricks to steal your money or information.
  • Phishing is when criminals pretend to be someone you trust.
  • They are often sent through email, text, or fake websites.
The aim is usually the same: to pressure you into clicking, paying, or giving away private details.

Why Scams & Phishing Matter

  • Most online crimes start with phishing emails.
  • Criminals use them to steal passwords, bank details, or identities.
  • Falling for scams can lead to financial loss and emotional stress.

Common Impact

  • Stolen accounts
  • Money lost
  • Personal distress

Why Awareness Helps

  • Helps you pause before clicking
  • Makes scams easier to spot
  • Reduces avoidable damage

How to Spot a Scam

  • Check the sender’s email address carefully.
  • Look for spelling mistakes or urgent language.
  • Be suspicious of unexpected links or attachments.
  • Real companies will not ask for passwords by email or text.
Scam messages often try to rush you, frighten you, or make you act before thinking.

What to Do if You’re Targeted

  • Do not click links or open attachments.
  • Report the email or text to report@phishing.gov.uk.
  • Block and delete suspicious messages.
  • Change your password immediately if you clicked.
  • Contact your bank if you gave financial details.
Acting quickly can limit the damage and help protect your accounts and money.

Examples of Common Scams

These examples are split clearly so the risky examples and safer reality checks are easier to see.

Common Scam Examples

  • Fake delivery texts asking for payment
  • Emails pretending to be your bank
  • Fake HMRC tax refund offers

Reality Check

  • Banks will not ask for passwords by email or text.
  • Government messages should always be checked carefully before responding.

Key Takeaways

  • Be cautious with unexpected messages.
  • Never give away personal details through links.
  • Report and delete suspicious emails or texts.
  • Change passwords quickly if you fall victim.
Slowing down and checking before you act is one of the best protections against scams.
