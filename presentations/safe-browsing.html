What is Safe Browsing

  • Using the internet in ways that protect your privacy and security.
  • Avoiding unsafe websites and risky downloads.
  • Being cautious when using public Wi-Fi networks.
Safe browsing means slowing down, checking what you are clicking on, and choosing trusted websites, apps, and connections.

Why Safe Browsing Matters

  • Unsafe browsing can expose you to viruses and scams.
  • Fake websites may steal personal or banking details.
  • Public Wi-Fi can allow hackers to intercept your data.

Common Risks

  • Viruses and malware
  • Fake websites
  • Data theft on public Wi-Fi

Why It Helps

  • Protects your personal data
  • Reduces scam exposure
  • Keeps accounts and devices safer

How to Browse Safely

  • Look for secure websites with https:// and a padlock symbol.
  • Only download apps or software from official stores.
  • Avoid clicking pop-ups and suspicious ads.
  • Use a VPN when on public Wi-Fi.
  • Log out of accounts after use, especially on shared devices.
Safer browsing comes from checking websites properly and avoiding quick clicks on things that feel off.

Examples of Unsafe Browsing

These examples are split clearly so the risky actions and safer habits are easy to compare.

Unsafe Examples

  • Entering bank details on a site without https://
  • Downloading free movies or software from shady websites
  • Using public Wi-Fi for online banking

Safer Examples

  • Using trusted shopping sites with reviews
  • Keeping your browser updated

Key Takeaways

  • Always check for secure connections (https://).
  • Download only from trusted sources.
  • Avoid sensitive activity on public Wi-Fi.
  • Keep browsers and apps updated.
A bit more caution while browsing goes a long way in avoiding scams, malware, and data theft.
