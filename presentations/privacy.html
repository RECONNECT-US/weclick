What is Privacy & Data Protection?

Privacy

  • Controlling who can see your personal information online
  • Managing what other people, apps, and websites can access

Data Protection

  • Protecting sensitive details like addresses, phone numbers, and ID information
  • Managing app and social media settings for safety
In simple terms, privacy and data protection are about keeping control of your information and making sure it is not shared, misused, or exposed unnecessarily.

Why Privacy & Data Protection Matters

  • Criminals can use personal information for fraud and identity theft.
  • Oversharing can put you or your family at risk.
  • Protecting privacy helps keep you safer in the digital world.

Real-World Risk

  • Fake accounts and scams
  • Impersonation
  • Financial or personal harm

Why It Helps

  • Reduces exposure
  • Improves confidence online
  • Supports safer decision-making

How to Protect Your Privacy

Good Privacy Habits

  • Check and update social media privacy settings
  • Be careful with what you share online
  • Turn off location tracking when it is not needed

Safer Digital Practice

  • Use encrypted messaging apps like Signal or WhatsApp
  • Keep personal and work accounts separate
Small changes in settings and habits can make a big difference to how much personal information is visible to others.

Examples of Privacy Risks

This format keeps the examples clean, easy to read, and clearly separated so nothing overlaps on screen.

Risky Examples

  • Posting holiday plans on social media
  • Sharing ID or bank information online
  • Using the same email for all accounts

Safer Examples

  • Adjusting Facebook or Instagram privacy settings
  • Using separate email addresses for personal and work use

Key Takeaways

  • Think before sharing personal details online.
  • Always review privacy settings on apps and social media.
  • Limit location tracking and unnecessary data collection.
  • Protecting your privacy helps prevent fraud and identity theft.
The goal is not to stop using technology. It is to use it more safely, with better awareness and stronger boundaries around your information.
