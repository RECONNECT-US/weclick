Device Help Support

This worksheet is for supporting learners with everyday device use across mobile phones, tablets, laptops and desktop computers. It can be used in sessions, drop-ins or general digital skills support.

Sample worksheet only: this page is for guidance and to show how the form is laid out. It does not collect, store or submit any personal information, and it cannot be filled in online.

Support Guide

  1. Show the learner the basic parts of the device, such as buttons, charging point, camera, keyboard, touch screen, or mouse.
  2. Help them practise simple actions like switching on, unlocking, opening apps, returning to the home screen, and closing things down properly.
  3. Go through useful basics such as Wi-Fi, volume, brightness, battery, and safe charging.
  4. Let the learner try each step themselves so confidence builds naturally instead of doing it all for them.
  5. Repeat key tasks slowly and clearly so the learner can recognise the same process next time.
  • Can the learner turn the device on and off?
  • Can the learner connect to Wi-Fi?
  • Can the learner open and close apps or programs?
  • Can the learner use the touch screen, keyboard, or mouse?
  • Can the learner recognise simple settings and basic controls?
Helpful reminder: device support can be adapted for phones, tablets, laptops and desktop computers. The aim is to make the learner more comfortable with the device they already have or are most likely to use.
