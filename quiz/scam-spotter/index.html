What learners practise

Spotting urgency, threats, fake prizes, and emotional manipulation
Checking suspicious links, unknown senders, and fake contact names
Recognising fake screenshots and fake image bait on WhatsApp
Telling the difference between a harmless message and one that deserves caution

How it works

Pick a starting level, answer the scenarios, and the app adapts as the learner improves. Do well and it gets tougher. Get stuck and it gives a gentler run.

Easy = obvious warning signs
Normal = more convincing scams
Hard = subtle, believable, mixed clues

How to use this

Read the message on screen, tap every warning sign you notice, then choose whether the whole message is probably safe or suspicious. Some safe messages appear in the mix so learners do not just press scam every time.

Look for pressure, urgency, secrecy, or threats
Check the sender details and the link carefully
Notice fake image tricks, fake proof, and fake attachment prompts
When unsure, do not click — verify another way
