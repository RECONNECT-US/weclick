Workplace Communication: A Case Study

Maria works as a support officer at a housing organisation. Her role involves responding to emails, managing complaints, completing digital forms, and speaking with external agencies. Recently, the organisation introduced a new digital reporting system. Although Maria feels confident with most tasks, she is unsure how to use the new system effectively.

During a team meeting, the manager explains that all staff will receive additional training. Maria asks several questions to ensure she understands the new procedures. Her manager praises her for showing initiative and encourages the team to raise any concerns early.

Maria wants to progress to a senior role in the future, so improving her professional communication is important for her development.

Session support

Use this panel as a quick guide during sessions. It does not change when you move between modules, so trainers always have a simple overview on the right side.

  • 💡 Whole group: Use the big screen, read questions out loud and let learners answer together before they choose their option.
  • 👥 1‑to‑1: Sit with the learner, repeat questions slowly and let them talk through why they chose an answer.
  • 🧱 Keep language simple. You can explain new words in your own words or in the learner’s first language if that helps.
Recommended order for new learners:
  1. Vocabulary  →  6–8 minutes
  2. Grammar  →  6–8 minutes
  3. Reading  →  8–10 minutes
  4. Spelling  →  6–8 minutes
