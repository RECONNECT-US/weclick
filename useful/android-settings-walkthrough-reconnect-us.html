Before you start

1

Look for the Settings app on the phone. It often looks like a cog or gear.

2

Tap it once to open it.

3

If you cannot see it, swipe up on the home screen to see all apps, then look for Settings.

Tip: Many phones have a search box at the top of Settings. If you are stuck, type a word like Wi-Fi, sound, Bluetooth, or brightness.

The quickest way to change common settings

1

Put your finger at the very top of the screen.

2

Swipe down once. You should see small buttons. This is often called Quick Settings.

3

Swipe down again to see more buttons.

4

Tap a button to turn it on or off. Common ones are Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Torch, Airplane mode, and Do Not Disturb.

On many Android phones, touching and holding one of these buttons opens the full settings page for that item.

Main parts of Android Settings

Internet and connections

Used for Wi-Fi, mobile data, Bluetooth, and hotspot.

Sound and notifications

Used for ringtone, media volume, alarms, and alerts.

Display

Used for brightness, screen timeout, dark mode, and text size.

Apps

Used to look at your apps, remove them, or change what they can use.

Security and privacy

Used for screen lock, fingerprints, permissions, and safety settings.

System / About phone

Used for updates, language, storage, date and time, and phone information.

Step-by-step: the most useful settings

1) Connect to Wi-Fi

SettingsNetwork & internetWi-Fi

  • Open Wi-Fi.
  • Make sure Wi-Fi is turned on.
  • Tap the name of the Wi-Fi you want.
  • Type the password if asked, then tap Connect.

2) Turn mobile data on or off

SettingsNetwork & internetSIMs / Mobile network / Data usage

  • Open the mobile data section.
  • Tap the switch for Mobile data to turn it on or off.

3) Change the volume

SettingsSound

  • Move the sliders left or right.
  • You may see separate sliders for Media, Calls, Ringtone, and Alarm.

4) Make the screen brighter or darker

SettingsDisplayBrightness

  • Move the brightness bar left for darker.
  • Move it right for brighter.

5) Make the writing bigger

SettingsDisplayFont size

  • Choose a bigger text size if the writing is hard to read.
  • Some phones also have Display size to make buttons and app items bigger.

6) Turn Bluetooth on

SettingsConnected devicesBluetooth

  • Turn on Bluetooth.
  • Wait for your phone to look for nearby devices.
  • Tap the device name, such as headphones or a speaker.

7) Set a screen lock

SettingsSecurity & privacyScreen lock

  • Choose PIN, Pattern, or Password.
  • Follow the steps on the screen.
  • Pick something safe that other people will not guess easily.

8) Change app permissions

SettingsAppsChoose an appPermissions

  • Open an app from the list.
  • Tap Permissions.
  • Choose what the app can use, such as camera, microphone, or location.

9) Check for software updates

SettingsSystemSoftware update

  • Tap Check for updates if that button appears.
  • Follow the instructions shown on the screen.

10) Learn about your phone

SettingsAbout phone

  • Here you can often find the phone model, Android version, storage, and other useful information.

SettingsAccessibility

If the phone looks different

  • Some Android phones use different names for the same settings.
  • One phone may say Network & internet, while another may say Connections.
  • One phone may say Security & privacy, while another may split those into two different sections.
  • If you cannot find something, use the search box at the top of Settings.
The search box is often the easiest way to find what you need.

Very simple practice task

1

Open Settings.

2

Tap the search box.

3

Type Wi-Fi.

4

Tap the result and look at the page.

5

Go back and try searching for Sound or Brightness.

Important note

This guide keeps things simple, so exact names may differ slightly depending on the phone brand and Android version. Quick Settings on Android is normally opened by swiping down from the top of the screen, many Quick Settings buttons can be opened further by touching and holding them, app permissions are commonly changed in Settings under Apps or Security and Privacy, and update details are often found under System or About phone. Those are standard Android help patterns, although some phone makers move them around a little.
