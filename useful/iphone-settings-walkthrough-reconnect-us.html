Before you start

1

Look for the Settings app on the iPhone. It usually looks like a grey cog or gear.

2

Tap it once to open it.

3

If you cannot see it, swipe down on the Home Screen and use the search box to type Settings.

Tip: iPhone Settings has a search box near the top. If you are stuck, type a word like Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, notifications, or brightness.

The quickest way to change common settings

1

On most newer iPhones, put your finger at the top right corner of the screen.

2

Swipe down to open Control Centre.

3

On older iPhones with a Home button, swipe up from the bottom instead.

4

Tap a control to turn it on or off. Common ones are Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Torch, Airplane Mode, and Focus.

On iPhone, many controls show more options if you touch and hold them.

Step-by-step: the most useful settings

1) Connect to Wi-Fi

SettingsWi-Fi

  • Open Wi-Fi.
  • Make sure Wi-Fi is turned on.
  • Tap the Wi-Fi name you want.
  • Type the password if asked, then tap Join.

2) Turn mobile data on or off

SettingsMobile Service or Cellular

  • Open the mobile data section.
  • Tap the switch for Mobile Data or Cellular Data.

3) Change the volume

SettingsSounds & Haptics

  • Move the volume slider left or right.
  • You can also change ringtone and other sound options here.

4) Make the screen brighter or darker

SettingsDisplay & Brightness

  • Move the brightness bar left for darker.
  • Move it right for brighter.

5) Make the writing bigger

SettingsDisplay & BrightnessText Size

  • Move the text slider to make words bigger.
  • You can also look in Accessibility for even larger text options.

6) Turn Bluetooth on

SettingsBluetooth

  • Turn on Bluetooth.
  • Wait for your iPhone to look for nearby devices.
  • Tap the device name, such as headphones or a speaker.

7) Change app permissions

SettingsPrivacy & Security

  • Open Privacy & Security.
  • Choose something like Camera, Microphone, Photos, or Location Services.
  • Turn app access on or off as needed.

8) Change notifications

SettingsNotifications

  • Tap an app from the list.
  • Choose whether that app is allowed to show notifications.

9) Check for software updates

SettingsGeneralSoftware Update

  • Open Software Update.
  • Follow the instructions shown on the screen if an update is available.

10) Learn about your iPhone

SettingsGeneralAbout

  • Here you can often find the iPhone model, software version, storage, and other useful information.

If the iPhone looks a bit different

  • Some iPhones open Control Centre differently depending on whether they have a Home button.
  • Some menu names can vary slightly depending on region, for example Mobile Service or Cellular.
  • If you cannot find something, use the search box at the top of Settings.
The search box is often the easiest way to find what you need.

Very simple practice task

1

Open Settings.

2

Tap the search box.

3

Type Wi-Fi.

4

Tap the result and look at the page.

5

Go back and try searching for Bluetooth or Brightness.

Important note

This guide keeps things simple, so exact names may differ slightly depending on the iPhone model and iOS version. Apple says Control Centre is opened by swiping down from the top-right on iPhones with Face ID, or swiping up from the bottom on older iPhones with a Home button. Apple also says many controls can be touched and held for more options, app permissions are managed in Settings under Privacy & Security, notification settings are changed in Settings under Notifications, and software details are found in Settings > General > About or Software Update.
