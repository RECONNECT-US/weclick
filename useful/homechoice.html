🏠 Guide to Kent Homechoice

Kent Homechoice is the system used to apply for social housing in Kent. This guide shows you how to register, understand your priority, and bid for homes.

What Kent Homechoice does:

It allows you to apply once, join a housing register, and bid for available homes instead of applying to each housing provider separately.

🌐 Step 1: Register

  1. Go to www.kenthomechoice.org.uk
  2. Click Register
  3. Fill in your personal details (name, address, date of birth)
  4. Enter your National Insurance number
  5. Add details about your household
  6. Provide income, benefits, and savings information
  7. Include any medical or support needs if relevant
  8. Upload or send documents (ID, tenancy agreements, etc.)

📊 Priority Bands

  • Band A: Urgent need
  • Band B: High need
  • Band C: Medium need
  • Band D: Low need

The higher your band, the better your chances.

🛡️ What Happens Next

  • Your council checks your application
  • You are told if you qualify
  • You are placed in a priority band

🏡 Step 2: Bidding for Homes

📅
Check Weekly Homes are listed Thursday–Tuesday
🔐
Log In Access your account
🏠
View Homes See what you qualify for
✔️
Bid Choose up to 3 homes per week

Bidding does not involve money — it shows interest only.

📦 Step 3: Offers & Moving

  • If you are selected, the landlord will contact you
  • You may be asked to view the property
  • If accepted, you will receive tenancy details
  • If refused, you stay on the register and can continue bidding

💡 Tips for Success

  • Log in every week
  • Keep your details up to date
  • Be realistic about waiting times
  • Ask for help if you get stuck

🤝 Need Help?

  • Visit: www.kenthomechoice.org.uk
  • Contact your local council housing team
  • Ask WECLICK staff for support
