🎣 Understanding Phishing

Learn how to spot scams and protect your personal information

❓ What is Phishing?

Phishing is when someone tries to trick you into giving away personal or financial information (like passwords, bank details, or ID numbers) by pretending to be a trusted organisation.

It often happens through emails, text messages, phone calls, or fake websites.

🚨 How to Spot a Phishing Attempt

  • Unexpected messages – emails or texts you weren’t expecting
  • Urgent or scary language – “Your account will be closed!”
  • Suspicious links or attachments – may lead to fake websites or viruses
  • Poor spelling and grammar – messages that look “off”
  • Too good to be true – prizes, refunds, or money out of nowhere

👀 Real vs Phishing Example

✅ Safer Message

From: support@yourbank.co.uk

“We’ve noticed unusual activity. Please log into your account using our official app or website.”

  • Recognisable sender
  • No pressure or panic
  • Directs you safely

⚠️ Phishing Message

From: secure-alert@bank-check-now.net

“URGENT! Your account is locked. Click here immediately to unlock it!”

  • Unknown sender
  • Pushes urgency
  • Contains suspicious link

🛡️ How to Protect Yourself

  • Stop and think before clicking anything
  • Check the sender carefully
  • Go directly to official websites instead of using links
  • Never share passwords or personal details by email or text
  • Keep your device updated and secure

🚫 What To Do If You Suspect Phishing

  • Do NOT reply
  • Do NOT click links or download attachments
  • Report it immediately:
    • Email: report@phishing.gov.uk
    • Text: 7726 (free)
  • Delete the message after reporting

💡 Remember

Phishing only works if you take the bait. Stay alert, double-check everything, and never share your details unless you are 100% sure.

⚠️ Quick Check Before You Click

  • Do I know the sender?
  • Am I being rushed or pressured?
  • Does this look genuine?

If in doubt — don’t click, don’t reply, don’t share.
