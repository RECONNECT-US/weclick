📧 Tips for Email & Text Messages

Stay safe, professional and clear when using emails and text messages.

🛡️ Safety First – Avoid Scams

  • Never click on links in messages from unknown senders.
  • Do not open attachments unless you are sure they are safe.
  • Watch for spelling mistakes, strange wording or urgent requests.
  • If unsure, contact the sender directly using a trusted phone number or email address.

🔒 Protect Your Personal Information

  • Never share bank details, passwords or ID numbers by email or text.
  • Avoid posting your email address or phone number in public places online.
  • Use strong, unique passwords for your email account.

👀 Good Message vs Risky Message Example

✅ Safer Message

From: known.contact@organisation.org

Message: “Hi, just confirming your appointment is at 2pm tomorrow. Please call us on our usual number if you need to rearrange.”

  • Known sender
  • Clear wording
  • No suspicious link
  • No pressure or threats

⚠️ Risky Message

From: unknown-alert247@mail-secure-now.net

Message: “URGENT! Your account will be closed today. Click here now to fix it immediately!”

  • Unknown sender
  • Pushy or urgent language
  • Suspicious wording
  • Tries to make you panic

💼 Be Professional

  • Use a clear subject line in emails.
  • Keep messages polite and easy to read.
  • Avoid slang or abbreviations that might be misunderstood.
  • Double-check spelling and grammar before sending.

✍️ Keep It Short & Clear

  • For texts, stick to the main point.
  • For emails, use short paragraphs and bullet points if needed.
  • Always state what action, if any, you need from the other person.

🤝 Respect Boundaries

  • Do not send messages late at night unless it is urgent.
  • Be careful who you “Reply All” to in emails.
  • Always get consent before adding someone to a group chat or mailing list.

🗂️ Keep Records

  • Save important emails or texts in case you need them later.
  • For work-related messages, use your work email or an approved messaging platform.

🚨 Report Suspicious Messages

  • If you think an email or text is a scam, do not reply.
  • Forward suspicious emails to: report@phishing.gov.uk
  • Forward scam texts to: 7726 (free service)

Remember: Think before you click, type or send — once a message is sent, it cannot be taken back.
