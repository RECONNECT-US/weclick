💬 Spotting Scammers on Social Media

Learn how to recognise and avoid scams in chats and messages

❗ Why This Matters

Scammers can appear friendly, professional, or even vulnerable. They build trust quickly to get your personal information or money.

If something feels off — trust your instincts.

👤 Check Their Profile

  • Very few posts or little personal history
  • New account with minimal followers or friends
  • Profile picture looks too perfect (may be stolen)
  • Details don’t match (location, job, age)

🗣️ Watch Their Behaviour

  • Strange grammar or unusual tone
  • Getting personal very quickly
  • Overly flattering or dramatic messages
  • Avoids answering direct questions

👀 Genuine vs Scam Conversation

✅ Genuine Chat

“Hi, just checking if you're still available for the session tomorrow.”

  • Clear and normal conversation
  • No pressure
  • No personal requests

⚠️ Scam Chat

“I feel like I’ve known you forever. I need help urgently, can you send money?”

  • Moves too fast emotionally
  • Requests money
  • Creates urgency

💰 Watch for Money Requests

  • Requests for bank details or transfers
  • Asking for gift cards
  • Emergency stories (medical, travel, etc.)
  • “I’ll send you money but you pay a fee first”

🔗 Be Careful with Links & Files

  • Suspicious or unrelated links
  • “Click now” urgency messages
  • Attachments from strangers
  • Could contain viruses or spyware

🎁 Too Good to Be True?

  • Easy money or job offers
  • Large prizes out of nowhere
  • Fast-moving romantic interest
  • Upfront payments required

⚠️ Manipulation Tactics

  • Urgency: “Act now or lose everything”
  • Emotional pressure: fear, love, sympathy
  • Isolation: “Don’t tell anyone about this”

🚫 What To Do

  • Stop responding immediately
  • Block and report the account
  • Do not share personal details
  • Take screenshots for evidence

💡 WECLICK Reminder

Your safety online is just as important as your safety in real life. Take your time, think clearly, and never feel pressured to respond.

✔️ Quick Check

  • Does this feel rushed or emotional?
  • Are they asking for money or details?
  • Does the profile look genuine?

If in doubt — stop, block, and report.
