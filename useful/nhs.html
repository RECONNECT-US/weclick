📱 Guide to Accessing the NHS App

The NHS App is a free and secure way to access NHS services online. This guide shows you what you need, how to download it, how to sign in, and how to stay safe while using it.

What you can use the NHS App for:

View health information, order repeat prescriptions, check test results, manage appointments, and view vaccination history.

✅ What You Will Need

  • A smartphone or tablet (Apple or Android), or a computer
  • An email address you can access
  • A mobile number that can receive text messages
  • Your NHS number if available
  • Photo ID such as a passport, UK driving licence, or EU ID card

⬇️ How to Download the App

  • Android: Open Google Play Store, search NHS App, then tap Install
  • iPhone: Open Apple App Store, search NHS App, then tap Get

🖥️ Accessing It on a Computer

If you do not want to use a phone or tablet, you can go to the NHS website and open the NHS App in your web browser. From there, you can sign in or create an account.

📝 Creating Your NHS Login

  1. Open the app or website
  2. Select Continue with NHS login
  3. Enter your email address and create a secure password
  4. Check your email and click the confirmation link
  5. Enter your mobile number
  6. Type in the security code sent by text

🪪 Proving Your Identity

You may need to prove who you are to unlock all features in the NHS App.

  • Have your photo ID ready
  • Take a photo of your ID when asked
  • Record a short video of your face saying 4 numbers

If you do not have photo ID, you may be asked questions about your health record instead. You may need your postcode, date of birth, and NHS number.

🔓 Signing In

  1. Open the app or website
  2. Enter your email and password
  3. Type in the security code sent to your phone
  4. You are now logged in

🛡️ Staying Safe Online

  • Never share your NHS login details
  • Always log out on shared devices
  • Keep your phone, tablet or computer locked with a PIN or password

🤝 Need Help?

If you get stuck, WECLICK can help step by step.

  • Visit the NHS help page for support
  • Call the NHS App Helpdesk on 0300 561 0244
  • Helpdesk hours: Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm
